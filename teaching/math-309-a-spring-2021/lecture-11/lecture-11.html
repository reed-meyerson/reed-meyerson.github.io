Matrix Exponential

If \(A=P\begin{pmatrix} \lambda_1 & 0\\ 0 & \lambda_2\end{pmatrix}P^{-1}\) then

\begin{equation*} e^{At}=P\begin{pmatrix} e^{\lambda_1t} & 0\\ 0 & e^{\lambda_2t}\end{pmatrix}P^{-1} \end{equation*}
  • eigenvalues can be real or complex (use Euler’s formula)

Goals for Today

  • Understand how to solve ODEs with the matrix exponential
  • Understand properties of matrix exponential

Matrix Exponential Solution

Claim: Let \(A\) be an \(n\times n\) matrix and \(x_0\in\mathbb{R}^n\). Then the solution to

\begin{equation*} \begin{cases} x'&=Ax\\ x(0)&=x_0 \end{cases} \end{equation*}

is \(x(t)=e^{At}x_0\).

Exercise

Convince yourself that \(x(t)=e^{At}x_0\) is a vector-valued function

Initial Condition

\begin{align*} x(t)&=e^{At}x_0\\ &=[\sum\limits_{k=0}^\infty \frac{A^kt^k}{k!}]x_0\\ &=[I+tA+\frac{t^2}{2}A^2+...]x_0\\ \end{align*}

So

\begin{align*} x(0)&=[I+0+0+...]x_0\\ &=Ix_0\\ &=x_0 \end{align*}

Derivative

\begin{align*} x'(t)&=\frac{d}{dt}[e^{At}x_0]\\ &=\frac{d}{dt}\sum\limits_{k=0}^\infty [t^k\frac{A^k}{k!}x_0]\\ &=\sum\limits_{k=0}^\infty \frac{d}{dt}[t^k\frac{A^k}{k!}x_0]\\ &=\sum\limits_{k=0}^\infty kt^{k-1}\frac{A^k}{k!}x_0\\ &=\sum\limits_{k=1}^\infty kt^{k-1}\frac{A^k}{k!}x_0\\ \end{align*}

Derivative Continued

\begin{align*} x'(t)&=\sum\limits_{k=1}^\infty kt^{k-1}\frac{A^k}{k!}x_0\\ &=\sum\limits_{k=1}^\infty A[kt^{k-1}\frac{A^{k-1}}{k!}x_0]\\ &=A\sum\limits_{k=1}^\infty [t^{k-1}\frac{A^{k-1}}{(k-1)!}x_0]\\ &=A[\sum\limits_{k=0}^\infty t^{k}\frac{A^{k}}{k!}]x_0\\ &=Ae^{At}x_0\\ &=Ax(t) \end{align*}

So \(x'=Ax\) as required.

Example

Let \(A=\begin{pmatrix} 1 & -3\\ 3 & 1\end{pmatrix}\). Solve the following IVP

\begin{equation*} \begin{cases} x'&=Ax\\ x(0)&=(1,5) \end{cases} \end{equation*}

Solution

\(A=\begin{pmatrix} i & -i\\ 1 & 1\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix} 1+3i & 0\\0 & 1-3i\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix} i & -i\\ 1 & 1\end{pmatrix}^{-1}\) So

\begin{equation*} x(t)=\begin{pmatrix} i & -i\\ 1 & 1\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix} e^{(1+3i)t} & 0\\0 & e^{(1-3i)t}\end{pmatrix}\begin{pmatrix} i & -i\\ 1 & 1\end{pmatrix}^{-1}\begin{pmatrix} 1\\ 5\end{pmatrix} \end{equation*}

Properties of the Matrix Exponential

  • \(e^{0_{n\times n}}=I_{n\times n}\)
    • (We showed this in our computation of “\(x(0)\)”)
  • The columns of \(e^{At}\) form a fundamental set of solutions to \(x'=Ax\).
  • \(e^{A(t+s)}=e^{At}e^{As}\)
  • \(e^{A}\) is invertible
    • \([e^{A}]^{-1}=e^{-A}\)
  • In general \(e^{A+B}\neq e^{A}e^{B}\)
    • However, if \(AB=BA\) then \(e^{A+B}=e^Ae^B\)

Columns of \(e^{At}\)

For a \(2\times 2\) system, a general solution to \(x'=Ax\) can be written as

\begin{equation*} e^{At}\begin{pmatrix} c_1\\ c_2\end{pmatrix} \end{equation*}

If \(e^{At}=(f_1(t)|f_2(t))\) where \(f_1:\mathbb{R}\to\mathbb{R}^2\) and \(f_2:\mathbb{R}\to\mathbb{R}^2\), then

\begin{equation*} e^{At}\begin{pmatrix} c_1\\ c_2\end{pmatrix}=c_1f_1(t)+c_2f_2(t) \end{equation*}

Columns of \(e^{At}\)

If \(e^{At}=(f_1(t)|f_2(t))\) then \(e^{0_{2\times 2}}=I_{2\times 2}\), so

\begin{align*} f_1(0)&=\begin{pmatrix} 1\\ 0\end{pmatrix}\\ f_2(0)&=\begin{pmatrix} 0\\ 1\end{pmatrix} \end{align*}

Columns of \(e^{At}\)

It follows that \(f_1,f_2\) satisfy the following IVPs

\begin{equation*} \begin{cases} f_1'&=Af_1\\ f_1(0)&=(1,0) \end{cases} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \begin{cases} f_2'&=Af_2\\ f_2(0)&=(0,1) \end{cases} \end{equation*}

\(e^{A(t+s)}=e^{At}e^{As}\)

Let \(x_0\in\mathbb{R}^n\), Let \(x(t)\) solve

\begin{equation*} \begin{cases} x'&=Ax\\ x(0)&=x_0 \end{cases} \end{equation*}

Let \(s\in\mathbb{R}\). Let \(\tilde x(t)\) solve

\begin{equation*} \begin{cases} \tilde x'&=A\tilde x\\ \tilde x(0)&=x(s) \end{cases} \end{equation*}

Then \(\tilde x(t)=x(t+s)\)

\([e^{A}]^{-1}=e^{-A}\)

\begin{align*} e^{A(1+(-1))}&=e^{A}e^{-A}\\ &=e^{0_{n\times n}}\\ &=I \end{align*}
